Start Here

This guide helps you document what you already see. No new framework to learn — just a structured way to capture participation patterns that protect students across every team, placement, and transition that follows.

You already have the data. The child who stayed engaged because the group was small. The one who communicated because the routine was familiar. The one who withdrew when the room got loud. This guide helps you write it down in a way the next team can use.

How This Guide Is Organized

Two PTKLF foundation areas organize this tool — Approaches to Learning and Social-Emotional Development. The DRDP aligns Approaches to Learning with Self-Regulation as a single strand (ATL-REG), reflecting how foundational regulation is to engagement and learning. Each section asks you to observe what you see in structured vs. less structured moments, and to document the conditions that made participation possible (or that made it break down).

What "Conditions" Means

Conditions are the specific features already present in every classroom — group size, adult proximity, visual structure, task demands, sensory load, routine predictability. When you notice that a child participates in one context but not another, the difference between those contexts is the finding.

Same child. Same week. Same classroom. She was engaged in small group with the visual schedule. She withdrew in loud free play without adult support. The variability isn't noise — it's data. Document what changed between those two moments.

How to Use This Instrument

Observation tab: Complete the Context Participation Matrix first. Then indicate whether participation appears generally consistent across routines or shifts meaningfully by context. If participation is consistent, the guide will generate a stable participation summary. If participation shifts by context, you'll document the contexts and conditions that explain the pattern.

Domains tab: Two indicator clusters — Approaches to Learning (including Self-Regulation) and Social-Emotional Development. Check each indicator that is consistently observed across routines. No re-scoring required — the Observation tab already captured the ecological record.

Synthesis tab: Complete the support profile, select observation scope, add teacher notes, then hit Generate.

A note on language: This guide uses participation language rather than deficit language. Instead of "cannot follow directions," you'll write "follows 2-step directions with visual supports in small group; needs adult prompting in whole-class contexts." That language protects your student — and tells the next team exactly where to start.

Documentation Language

How you write what you've observed shapes how every future team understands this child. Participation language is more accurate — and more protective — than deficit language.

One sentence worth keeping in every report:
"Skills are present but not consistently accessible under typical classroom demands."
This tells the reader the child has capacity, the environment is part of the equation, and what specialized instruction is targeting — without pathologizing the child or inflating eligibility.

Key Language Shifts

❌ Deficit Language

"Student has difficulty attending during circle time and cannot follow multi-step directions."

Locates the problem entirely in the child. Tells next team nothing about what works.

✓ Participation Language

"In circle time with adult nearby and visual schedule present, student sustains attention for 8–10 minutes and follows 2-step directions. In whole-group without visual supports, attention drops to 2–3 minutes and adult prompting is needed."

Documents what conditions enable access. Next team knows where to start.

❌ Deficit Language

"Student does not initiate peer interaction and withdraws during free play."

Describes the absence without explaining why or when.

✓ Participation Language

"In adult-facilitated small group with familiar peers, student initiates communication and responds to peer bids. In open free play with 8+ children, student watches from the edges and communication drops to gestures. Adult mediation is the enabling condition."

Describes the pattern and names what makes participation possible.

❌ Deficit Language

"Student has emotional regulation difficulties and frequently becomes dysregulated."

Vague, pathologizing, and useless for planning.

✓ Participation Language

"Student self-regulates successfully in predictable routines with visual schedule and adult within reach. Dysregulation occurs during unannounced transitions and in high-sensory environments (lunch, outdoor play). Recovery time is 5–8 minutes with co-regulation support from familiar adult."

Specific, plannable, and tells the next team exactly what to do.

From Teacher Observation to Present Levels

Your observations become the IEP's present levels. When the SLP, psychologist, or special education teacher writes the present levels section, they're drawing on exactly what you've documented here. The more specific your context notes, the stronger the present levels — and the more protected the student is when the team changes.

Policy & Practice Alignment

PRISM is designed to interoperate with the statutory, accountability, and professional practice frameworks that California early childhood teams already operate within. It does not create new requirements — it provides documentation architecture that fits the shape of systems already in place.

Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) — Part B

20 U.S.C. § 1400 et seq. — federal statutory foundation for special education

IDEA Part B requires that children with disabilities receive a free appropriate public education in the least restrictive environment. LRE determinations must be grounded in individualized analysis of each child's ability to participate in and benefit from education alongside peers with appropriate supports. PRISM's ecological observation logic — documenting what conditions enable participation before considering placement — is directly consistent with IDEA's participation-first mandate.

LRE Mandate FAPE Participation-First

IDEA Part B Indicator 6 & OSEP Results-Driven Accountability (RDA)

IDEA Part B State Performance Plan/Annual Performance Report — Indicator 6: Preschool LRE; OSEP Results-Driven Accountability Framework

IDEA Part B Indicator 6 requires states to report preschool placement data reflecting the extent to which children with disabilities participate in regular early childhood programs. OSEP's Results-Driven Accountability framework links these data to broader child outcomes and state determinations. PRISM supports defensible Indicator 6 decision-making by documenting environmental access, universal supports, and participation patterns prior to placement changes. By foregrounding ecological analysis, it strengthens alignment between classroom observation and LRE reporting requirements. PRISM is a documentation integrity tool — it produces the ecological record that makes placement decisions defensible, not a tool that optimizes placement percentages.

Indicator 6 Results-Driven Accountability Documentation Integrity LRE Reporting

ECTA Center — Preschool LRE Decision-Making

Early Childhood Technical Assistance Center (ECTA) — federally funded IDEA technical assistance

ECTA guidance emphasizes environmental analysis over categorical severity in preschool placement and service decisions. Rather than asking how impaired a child is, ECTA-aligned practice asks whether the environment provides access — and what supports enable meaningful participation alongside peers. ECTA's Guiding Questions for Discussing Services in the LRE and Preschool LRE Reference Points and Discussion Prompts are the closest federal TA analog to this observation framework — team discussion tools built around the same environmental access logic.

Preschool LRE Environmental Analysis Inclusive Practices IDEA Part B / Part C

DEC Recommended Practices

Division for Early Childhood, Council for Exceptional Children (2014)

DEC Recommended Practices emphasize natural environments, embedded instruction, and environmental supports as primary mechanisms of access. PRISM's participation-focused observation logic is consistent with DEC's emphasis on context, relationships, and functional skill use within daily routines. DEC Recommended Practices represent the national professional organization standard for early childhood special education — providing portability beyond California's state frameworks to teams in other districts and SELPAs.

Natural Environments Embedded Instruction Routine-Based Practice

CDE Inclusive Early Education & Rightful Presence

California Department of Education (2024)

CDE guidance defines inclusion as belonging and access within community settings — treating the environment as the primary locus of change, not the child. This guide's documentation approach is directly aligned with Rightful Presence principles: the question is always "does this environment provide access?" before asking "what does this child need to fix?"

Changelog

Version history for PRISM — Participation Routines & Inclusive Supports Map.

v1.1 — March 2026

Null / Stable Pathway

Added a Participation Variability Gate between the Context Participation Matrix and Forced Contrast. When participation is documented as generally consistent, the guide routes to a stable output pathway — no forced contrast, no driver documentation, no skills-protection sentence. Output: two sentences + scope. Prevents ecological signal inflation for typical participation profiles.

Soft Consistency Nudge

Non-blocking prompt that fires when the gate selection appears inconsistent with matrix data — e.g., "No variability" selected but matrix shows mixed access levels, or vice versa. Does not block generation; prompts review only.

Matrix as Single Source of Truth

Domain Pattern Classification removed from Synthesis tab. Participation pattern (stable / context-dependent-struct / context-dependent-ind / high-support / insufficient-data) is now derived directly from the Context Participation Matrix access column using deterministic precedence rules. No manual re-classification, no contradiction possible between observation and output.

Individualized Support Type — Bug Fix

Removed redundant "Individualized support type" radio that duplicated information already captured by the ind-support checkboxes. Support type is now derived directly from which boxes are checked (communication system > proximity > prompting/sensory plan), eliminating the double-ask and the contradiction risk.

Renderer v1.2 — P1/P2 Pipeline

Rebuilt output renderer as an explicit ordered array: P1 → P2 (conditional) → domain tail (conditional) → closing (conditional) → scope. P2 and audit trail appear only when divergence gates fire. Neutral universal opener in P1; label-aware sentence 2 derived from pattern classification. Closing sentence ("Skills are present but not consistently accessible...") suppressed on null pathway and selectively on high-support pathway.

Navigation Fix

Replaced global event.target dependency in show() with data-section attribute lookup. Resolves tab navigation failure in strict browser contexts.

Policy & Practice Alignment — Restructured

Renamed from "Framework Alignment" to "Policy & Practice Alignment." Card sequence reordered to statutory → accountability → federal TA → professional practice → state policy → state frameworks → state assessment → operational tool. Added IDEA Indicator 6 / OSEP RDA card (documentation integrity framing) and DEC Recommended Practices card. ECA demoted to research footnote.
